\section{The general setting}

Assume $H$ is a subgroup of $G$,
Suppose that $V$ is a $G$ module, $W$ is a irreducible $H$ module, such that
$\Hom_{H}(V,W)\neq 0$.
Define a $H$-module \[
\Omega_V(W) = V / \cnn_{V,W},
\]
where 
\[
\cnn_{V,W} = \bigcap_{T\in \Hom_H(V,W)}\Ker(T).
\]
This is a generization
of Howe quotient. It is clear that, any $S\in \Hom_H(V,V)$ act on 
$\Omega_V(W)$ via the quotient map,
the action is well defined by the construction
(For any $v\in \cnn_{V,W}$, $T\circ S(v) = 0$ since $T\circ S\in \Hom_H(V,W)$ 
for any $T\in \Hom_H(V,W)$. Hence $S(v)\in \cnn_{V,W}$.).
One crucial example is the $\cuu(\fgg)^K$ action on the 
$K$ isotypic component $V(\tau)\cong \Omega_V(W)$ of 
$V$ where $W\in \tau$.

One key property of $\Omega_V(W)$ is that:
\[
\Hom_H(V,W) \cong \Hom_H(\Omega_V(W),W).
\]

\begin{lemma}
Suppose that $\dim \Hom_{H}(V,W)=1$,
Then $S \in \Hom_{H}(V,V)$ act on $\Omega_{V,W}$ by a  scaler $c_{V,W}(S)$.
The scaler is determined by the $S$ action on any vector $v\in V$ with 
non-zero $H$-equivariant projection to $W$.
\end{lemma}
\proof
Since $T\circ S \in \Hom_{H}(V,W)$ for any $S\in \Hom_{H}(V,V)$ assumption,
$T\circ S = c_{V,X}(S)T$ by the assumption. 
Hence $S$ act on $\Omega_{V,W}$ by this scaler $c_{V,W}(S)$.
In fact, let $\phi\colon V\to \Omega_V(W)$ be 
the projection map, if $v\in V$ such 
that $\phi(v) \neq 0$, $S \phi(v) = \phi \circ S (v) = c_{V,W}(S) \phi(v)$. 
\qed


Consider the situation of see-saw pair:
Suppose we have see-saw pair
\[
\xymatrix{G \ar@{-}[dr] & H'\\
H\ar@{-}[ur] & G'
}
\]
where $H < G$, $H'> G'$. 

For representation $\sigma \in R(\omega,G')$ of $G'$, i.e. which can be realized as a quotient of
the Weil representation $\omega$, we have
\[
 \Omega_\omega(\sigma) = \Theta(\sigma)\otimes \sigma
\]
for some representation $\Theta(\sigma)$ of $G$ as $G\times G'$ representation.

For any irreducible representation $\tau$ of $H$.
\[
\begin{split}
&\Hom_{H}(\Theta(\sigma), \tau)\\
\cong & \Hom_{H\times G'}(\Theta(\sigma)\otimes \sigma, \tau\otimes \sigma)
\cong  \Hom_{H\times G'}(\omega/\cnn_\sigma, \tau\otimes \sigma)\\
\cong & \Hom_{H\times G'}(\omega, \tau\otimes \sigma)\\
\cong & \Hom_{H\times G'}(\omega/\cnn_\tau, \tau\otimes \sigma)
\cong \Hom_{H\times G'}(\tau\otimes \Theta(\tau), \tau\otimes \sigma)\\
\cong & \Hom_{G'}(\Theta(\tau), \sigma).
\end{split}
\]

Again assume $\dim \Hom_{H}(\Theta(\sigma),\tau) = 1$.
Since $\Theta(\sigma)$ is $\fgg$ module,  $\cuu(\fgg)^H$ act 
on $\Omega_{\Theta(\sigma),\tau} \cong \tau$ via character $c_{\Theta(\sigma),\tau}$.
Similary, since we have $\dim \Hom_G'(\Theta(\tau),\sigma) = 1$ by above,
$\cuu(\fhh')^{G'}$ action on $\Omega_{\Theta(\tau),\sigma}\cong \sigma$
via $c_{\Theta(\tau),\sigma}$.

{\bf Remark:}  $\cuu(\fgg)^H$ and $\cuu(\fhh')^{G'}$ 
has a joint action on $\Hom_{H\times G'}(\omega,\tau\otimes\sigma)$ and by above 
isomorphisms we can link the $\cuu(\fgg)^H$ action on $\Hom_H(\Theta(\sigma),\tau)$ and $\cuu(\fhh')^{G'}$  action on $\Hom_G'(\Theta(\tau),\sigma)$.

\begin{lemma}\label{lemma:ugkcorr}
For any see-saw pair, 
\[
\omega(\cuu(\fgg)^H) = \omega(\cuu(\fhh')^{G'}).
\]
Hence for any $x\in \cuu(\fgg)^H$ we can choose $y\in \cuu(\fhh')^{G'}$ 
such that $\omega(x) = \omega(y)$ and this choose is independent of real form.
\end{lemma}
\proof Clear from the Fock model of oscillate representation~\cite{Adams2007} 
and Howe's paper~\cite{Howe1989Rem}. See appendix.
\qed

\begin{lemma}
The map $c_{\Theta(\tau),\sigma}$ and $c_{\Theta(\sigma),\tau}$ determine each other 
and is independent of real form.
\end{lemma}
\proof 
Consider following commutative digram:
\[
\xymatrix{
\omega \ar[r]^{S_2} \ar[d]_{S_1}\ar[dr]^{Q} & \tau\otimes \Theta(\tau)\ar[d]^{T_2}\\
\Theta(\sigma)\otimes \sigma\ar[r]_{T_1} & \tau\otimes \sigma 
}
\]
Here $T_i = \phi_i\otimes \id$.
$\phi_1$ and $\phi_2$ are the projection map $\Theta(\sigma)\to \Omega_{\Theta(\sigma)}(\tau)\cong \tau$ 
and $\Theta(\tau)\to \Omega_{\Theta(\tau)}(\sigma)\cong \sigma$. $S_i$ are defined similarly.

Note that $\omega(\cuu(\fgg)^H) = \omega(\cuu(\fhh')^{G'})$,
for $x\in \cuu(\fgg)^H$, 
there is a $y\in \cuu(\fhh')^{G'}$.
such that $\omega(x) = \omega(y)$.


Now for any $v\otimes w\in \tau\otimes \sigma$, there is $a\in \omega$ such
that $Q(a) = v\otimes w$.
Hence, 
\[
c_{\Theta(\sigma),\tau}(x) (v\otimes w )
(x \cdot v)\otimes w 
= T_1 (x S_1 (a))
= T_1 \circ S_1 (\omega(x) a)
= T_2 \circ S_2 (\omega(y) a)
= v\otimes (y\cdot w).
= c_{\Theta(\tau),\sigma}(y)(v\otimes w)
\]
\qed


\section{The lifting of character}
Let us consider a simple case. Suppose $(G,G')$ are reductive dual pair. 
$V = \theta_{G', G}(\chi)$ is the theta lifting of character $\chi$ of $\tG'$.

The following result is crucial, and is known by \cite{Shimura1990}, 
\cite{Zhu2003} or \cite{Wallach1992real}:

We need a slightly generized version. 
\begin{lemma}\label{lemma:scalerk}
  Consder pair $(G,H)$ where 
  $H$ is a subgroup $G$ meet all the component of $G$.
  Suppose that $\fgg$ be the complexification of 
  a classical Lie algebra.
  Moreover there is a involution, such that $\fgg$ decomposite into 
  $\fgg = \fkk+\fpp$ where 
  $\fkk$ is the complexification of the Lie algebra of $H$.
  
  Let $\sigma$ be a one-dimensional representation of $H$.
  Let $\cjj = \Ker(\sigma)|_{\cuu(\fkk)}$.
  Then the natural map
  \[
  \czz(\fgg) \to \cuu(\fgg)^H/(\cjj\cuu(\fgg)\cap\cuu(\fgg)^H)
  \]
  is surjective.
\end{lemma}
\proof In view of Shimura's proof, the only step use the assumption 
that $G$ is classical is to grantee the following relation:
\[
\phi(S^r(\fgg)^G))+\cuu(\fgg)\fkk \supset \phi(S^r(\fgg)^H).
\]
But this is true in our case.So the above lemma
holds by the same proof. 
 \qed

\begin{lemma}
In above setting, let $V$ be a $G$ module and $(\chi,W)$ be a character of $H$
such that $\dim \Hom_H(V,W)=1$. 
Then the $\cuu(\fgg)^H$ action on $\Omega_{V,W}$ is determined by the
$Z(\fgg)$ action on $V$ and the  ideal $\cjj\subset \cuu(\fkk)$ of 
$\chi$.
\end{lemma}
\proof
Consider the $H$-module short exact sequence:
\[
0\to \cnn_{V,W}\to V\to^{\pi} W\to 0.
\]
For any $x\in \cuu(\fgg)^H$, choose $z\in Z(\fgg)$ such that 
$x-z \in \cjj\cuu(\fgg)$.
Note that for any $v\in V$ we have $jv \in \cnn_{V,W}$ for all $j\in \cjj$.
So $\pi \circ x -\pi \circ z = \pi \circ (x-z) = 0$.
Hence $c_{V,W}(x) = c_{V,W}(z)$.
\qed

\begin{thm}\label{thm:ugkcor}
Let $\chi \in R(G', \omega)$ be a character of $G'$. 
Suppose that $\tau \in R(H, \omega)$ such that 
$\dim \Hom_{H}(\Theta(\chi),\tau) = 1$.
Then the $\cuu(\fgg)^H$ action on $\Omega_{\Theta(\chi),\tau}$ 
is determined by the infinitesimal character of $\tau$ and annilater ideal 
$\cjj = \Ker(\chi)|_{\cuu(\fgg')}$  
\end{thm}
\proof
By Lemma~\ref{lemma:ugkcorr}, 
for any $x \in \cug^H$ we can find $x'\in \cuu(\fhh')^{G'}$. Such that
$\omega(x) = \omega(x')$ and this choice is independent of real form.
Now choose $z' \in \czz(\fhh')$ such that
$x'-z' \in \cjj\cuu(\fhh')$ by Lemma~\ref{lemma:scalerk}.

Then again by Lemma~\ref{lemma:ugkcorr}, we can find 
$z\in \czz(\fkk)$
such that $\omega(z) = \omega(z')$.
%So $x$ action on $\Omega_{\Theta(\sigma),\tau}$ is same as $z$ action on $\tau$.
Hence, $c_{\Theta(\sigma),\tau}(x) = \chi_{\tau}(z)$, 
where $\chi_{\tau}$ is the infinitesimal character of $\tau$.
\qed


Let $\Gamma = \Gamma_{\fgg,M}^{\fgg,K^c}$, where $M$ is 
the maximal compact subgroup of $H$, and $K^c$ is a compact real form of $\fkk$.
We abuse of notation and write $\Gamma = \Gamma_{\fkk,M}^{\fkk,K^c}$ when it apply to $(\fkk,M)$-module.
\begin{lemma}\label{lemma:derugkact}
Let $V$ be a $(\fgg,M)$-module, 
For $x\in \cuu(\fgg)^H = \cuu(\fgg)^{K^c}$, it act on 
$\Gamma^iV$ by $\Gamma^i x$, where view $x$ as a $(\fkk,M)$-module endomorphism
of $V$. 
\end{lemma}
\proof Form the realization of derived functor~(\cite{Wallach1988} Chapter 6).
\qed

\begin{lemma}\label{lemma:derugkact2}
In above setting, assume $\Gamma^iV \to \Gamma^i W$ is non-zero 
and $K^c$ type $\mu$ occure in the image
of this map.
Then $\Gamma^iV$ has a composition component with $\cuu(\fgg)^{K^c}$
action on the $K^c$ isotropic space of type $\mu$ via $c_{V,W}$. 
\end{lemma}
\proof
Consider the following diagram of $H$-module for $x\in \cuu(\fgg)^\tK$:
\[
\bfig
\iiixii{15}[\cnn_{V,W}`V`W`\cnn_{V,W}`V`W;``x`x`c_{V,W}(x)``]
\efig
\]
Take derive functor on it we get:
\[
\bfig
\hSquares[\Gamma^i\cnn_{V,W}`\Gamma^iV`\Gamma^iW`\Gamma^i\cnn_{V,W}`\Gamma^iV`\Gamma^iW;``\Gamma^ix`\Gamma^ix`c_{V,W}(x)``]
\efig
\]
So $\Gamma^i(V)(\mu)$ has a $\cuu(\fgg)^{K^c}\times {K^c}$ composition component
with $c_{V,W}\otimes \tau$ action.
By the equivalence of categories of $\cuu(\fgg)^{K^c}\times {K^c}$
module and $G$ module, the result is clear. 
\qed

\begin{cor}
  Suppose that 
  $G$ and $\ckG$ are two real forms of a reductive Lie algebra $\fgg$ as
  the setting in~\cite{WallachZhu2004}
  (i.e. there are Cartan involutions 
  and complex conjugations respect these real forms 
  are commute to each other).
  Moreover
  $(G,G')$ and $(\ckG,\ckG')$ form reductive dual pairs such that 
  $G'$ and $\ckG'$ are also two real forms of a same complex 
  Lie algebra $\fgg'$.
  
  Let $H$ be the subgroup of $G$ with Lie algebre $\cfkk$, where
  $\fgg=\cfkk \oplus \check{\fpp}$ under the involution of $\ckG$.

  Let $\Gamma = \Gamma_{H\cap \ckK}^{\ckK}$.
  Suppose 
  \begin{enumerate}[(1)]
  \item $\chi_1$ and $\chi_2$ are characters of $G$ and $\ckG$ respectively.
  \item  Let $\cjj_i = \Ker(\chi_i|_{\cuu(\fgg')}) \subset \cuu(\fgg')$.
    $\cjj_1 = \cjj_2$.
  \item $\tau$ is a $H$-module such that
    $\dim \Hom_{H}(\Theta(\chi_1),\tau_1) = 1$.
  \item $\mu$ is a $\ckK$-module occure in the
    image of map $\Gamma^i \Theta(\sigma_1) \to \Gamma^i \tau_1$ 
    such that 
    $\dim \Hom_{\ckK}(\Theta(\sigma_2),\tau_2) =1$.
  \end{enumerate}
  Then $\Gamma^i \Theta(\chi_1)$ and 
  $\Theta(\chi_2)$ has a isomrophic irreducible subquotient 
  with common $\ckK$-type $\mu$.
\end{cor}
\proof
By Lemma~\ref{lemma:derugkact2}, $\Gamma^i\pi$ has a composition component 
with $\cuu(\fgg)^{\ckK}$ action on $\tau_2$ component via $c_{\Theta(\chi_1),\tau_1}$.
By Theorem~\ref{thm:ugkcor}, for any $x\in \cuu(\fgg)^{\ckK}$, 
we can find $z\in Z(\fkk)$ such that $c_{\Theta(\chi_1),\tau}(x) = \chi_{\tau}(z)$.
On the other hand, $x$ act on $\mu$ isotropic space of $\Theta(\chi_2)$ 
is by $c_{\Theta(\chi_2),\mu}(x) = \chi_\mu(z)$.
Since the derived functor preserve the infinitesimal character, 
\[
c_{\Theta(\chi_1),\tau}(x) = \chi_\tau(z) = \chi_\mu(z)=c_{\Theta(\chi_2),\mu}(x).
\]
So by the equivalence of categories
the result follows.  
\qed

\section{General setting}

I would like to prove that the transfer of theta lifting of some module is again a theta lifting of same module of same group to different pairs. 
This includes the case of lifting of lowest weight module.



Suppose that 
Suppose $(\ckG,\ckG')$ is another real form.
$L(\mu)$ has non-zero lifting from $\ckM'$, and $\theta(L(\mu))$ has non-zero cohomology. There is a integer $r$, such that 
$\Gamma^r\theta(L(\mu)) \cong \mu$.
In fact the minimal integer such that $\Gamma^i\theta(L(\mu))$ non-zero will do.


\begin{thm}
Let $\mu$ be a irreducible representation of $K$ and $L(\mu)$ be the
represntation of theta lifing of $\mu$ to $M'$. 

Fix following data:
\begin{enumerate}[(a)]
\item a complex dual pair $\gg,\gg'$ in $\sp$;
\item two commuting involuton $\tau, \check{\tau}$ of $\sp$ preserve $\gg$
  and $\gg'$;
\end{enumerate}
Now let
\begin{enumerate}[(a)]
\item $\kk = \gg^{\tau}$;
\item $\mm$ be the dual of $\kk$ in $\sp$;
\item $G,G',K,M$ be the real form of $\gg,\gg',\kk,\mm$ with cartan involution $\tau$
\item $\check{G},\check{G}',\check{K},\check{M}$ be the real form of $\gg,\gg',\kk,\mm$ with cartan involution 
$\check{\tau}$(note that $\check{K}$ is not compact).
\end{enumerate}
 
Now assume the
occurence condition:
\begin{enumerate}[(iv)]
\item $\sigma$ is a $G'$ quotient of $L(\mu)$;
\item $\sigma$ occur in both of pairs $(G,G')$ and $(\check{G},\check{G'})$;
\end{enumerate}
Then
$\Gamma^r\Theta_{G}(\sigma)$ has a subquotient isomorphic to a subquotient of 
$\Theta_{\ckG}(\sigma)$ with same $K$ type $\mu$.
\end{thm}

\proof
Fix $\gg$,$\gg'$, $\kk$ and $\mm$ in $\sp$. 
Note that 
\[
\omega(U(\mm))^{G'}= \omega(U(g))^K 
\]
is independent of real form.  $\sigma$ has non-zero lifting since
$L(\mu)$ has nonzero quotient to $\sigma$.  The $\sU(g)^K$ action on
$Hom_K(\Theta(\sigma),\mu)$ is determined by $\sU(\mm)^{G'}$ same as
$Hom_K(\Gamma^i\Theta(\sigma),\Gamma^i\Theta(L(\mu))$.

\qed

\section{Cartan involution and real form}
Fix a involution $\theta$ on $\gg$.
Let $\kk$ be the $1$-eigenspace and $\fpp$ be the $-1$-eigenspace $\theta$.

To find a real form of $\gg_0$ has Cartan involution $\theta$ is to give a 
involution $\sigma$ on $\gg$, commute with $\theta$
and the $1$ eigenspace $\uu_0$ of $\tau = \sigma\theta$ is a compact real form
of $\gg$.
Take a compact real form $\uu_0$, such that $\gg = \uu_0\oplus i\uu_0$. 
Let $\tau_0$ be the Cartan involution respect to $\uu_0$ on $\gg_\bR$.
By Theorem~6.16 in \cite{Knapp1996Lie}:
\begin{thm}
Let $\gg_0$ be a real semisimple Lie algebra, let $\theta$ be
a Cartan involution, and let $\sigma$ be any involution.
Then there exists $\phi\in \mathrm{Int}\gg_0$ such that 
$\phi\theta\phi^{-1}$ commutes with $\sigma$.
\end{thm}
we can choose $\tau_0$ commute with $\theta$.  Now $\uu_0$ is stable
under $\theta$. Let $\kk_0 = \uu_0\cap \kk$ and $\pp_0=\uu_0\cap \pp$.
Then $\gg_0 = \kk_0\oplus \pp_0$ is the $1$-eigenspace of $\tau_0\theta$ and is a real form of $\gg$ with Cartan
involution $\theta$.
$\gg_0$ is a real Lie subalgebra of $\gg$ since $\theta$ and $\tau_0$ both
are involutions compatible with Lie structure. It is also clear by definition that $\gg_0\otimes \bC = \gg$.

\section{Remarks of models of oscillate representation}
This remarks are following Adams' notes~\cite{Adams2007} and Howe~\cite{Howe1989Rem}.
The main point is in the examples.

Take a complex vector space $X$, form a symplectic space $W = X\oplus X^*$.
Let $S = \bC[[X]]$ be the set of formal power series on $X$. 
Let $X$ act on $S$ by multiplication, and $X^*$ act on $S$ by differentiation as 
Adams~\cite{Adams2007} (twisted by $i=\sqrt{-1}$). 
There is a conical isomorphism of Lie algebra $\sp(W)\cong X\otimes X^* \oplus S^2(X) \oplus S^2(X^*)$.
And $\cuu(\sp(W))$ act on $S=\bC[[X]]$ as polynomial coeffients differential operators.

Now Take a real symplectic subspace $W_0$ of $W$ such that $(W_0)_\bC = W$. 
We can view $\sp(W_0)$ as a real subalgebra of $\sp(W)$ act on $\bC[[X]]$. 

The we can pick a symplectic basis $\Set{e_j,f_j}$ of $W_0$.  There is
a unique (upto scaler) $0\neq v\in S$ such that $(e_j-if_j)\cdot v =
0$.  The $W$ invariant subspace generated by $v$ is a model of
oscillator representation of $\sp(W_0)$.

\begin{lemma}
Fix a is a pair of complex Lie algebra $(\fgg,\fgg')$ of some dual pair.
For any real form $(G,G')$, we can find some $W_0$ 
such that $Lie(G) \cong \sp(W_0)\cap \fgg$ and $Lie(G')\cong \sp(W_0)\cap \fgg'$.
\end{lemma}

Let's look at a example. 
$U$ is a complex orthognal space with orthonormal basis $\Set{a_i}$. $V$ is a complex symplectic space with 
symplectic basis $\Set{b_i, c_i}$ where $b_i$ 
span a maximal isotropic subspace and $c_i$ are the corresponding dual vectors.
Let $W = U\otimes V$ and $X = \sspan\Set{a_i\otimes b_j}$. 
Denote $\fmm = X\otimes X^* \subset \sp(W)$.
$\fgg=\fso(U)$ and $\fgg'=\sp(V)$ be the subalgebra of $\sp(W)$.

For integer $p+q = \dim U$ and $2(r+s) = \dim V$,
let 
\begin{align*}
u_j =&
\begin{cases}
 a_j & j \leq p\\
i a_j & j > p,
\end{cases}
&
e_j = &
\begin{cases}
i b_j - c_j & j\leq r\\
b_j - i c_j & j > r,
\end{cases}
&
f_j = &
\begin{cases}
b_j - ic_j & j\leq r\\
ib_j -  c_j & j > r,
\end{cases}
\end{align*}
Let $W_0 = \sspan\Set{ u_j\otimes e_j, u_j\otimes f_j}$.
One check that $\fgg\cap \sp(W_0) = \fso(p,q)$,
$\fgg'\cap \sp(W_0) = \sp(2n)$ and $\fmm\cap \fgg\cap \sp(W_0) = \fuu(r,s)$.


{\bf Fock model}
If $\dim U = 1$, $p=1,q=0$  $r=\dim V$ and $s = 0$.
Let $W_0$ as above, then $e_j-if_j$ nil vector is $1\in \bC[[X]]$ 
and corresponding $W$ invariant $\bC[X]$ generated by $1$ is $\bC[X]$, this just the Fock model.

{\bf Schrodinger model}
On the other hand let $W$ be a complex symplectic space with symplectic basis $\Set{e_j,f_j}$.
Let $W_0$ be the real span of this basis. Then $e_j-if_j$ nil vector is $e^{-x^2/2}$ 
and this leads to the Schrodinger model.
